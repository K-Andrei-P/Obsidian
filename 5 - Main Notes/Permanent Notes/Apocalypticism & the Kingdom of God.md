# Apocalypticism & the Kingdom of God

Apocalypticism can be defined as the mother of christian theology. 

Apocalyptic prophecies emerged in times of deep suffering:
- Endless War
- Famine
- Disease

People became desperate and started hoping.

[[The apocalyptic prophecies from the old testament]] and the new testament are fundamentally different in their message.

Before Jesus, apocalyptic prophecies turned into prophecies like John's, where people lived in oppression and severe environments. People believed the world was too broken to fix and only divine intervention can change its course.

There were two views people saw these prophecies:
1. Restauration of the ideal past
2. Radical change of everything they have ever known

The blueprint was the same:
1. A radical shift in power in favor of God
2. God becomes the judge of both the dead and the living
3. This required a resurrection of the dead


This blueprint has flaws in it:
- People started giving up on the world, because why bother trying if it is going to get changed anyway: pessimism, resignation
- Anarchism: People started forcing God's hand

Jesus flipped the Message:
**Yours IS the Kingdom of God**

This meant that the Kingdom of God was already amongst the people, through [[the teaches and miracles of Jesus]].

This can be imaged like a growing tree.

The sapling is placed but has to be taken care of in order for it to grow.
Taking care of it means:
- Nächstenliebe
- Belief and Hope
- Prayer
- Changing Life in desperate times

--> Helping the Kingdom of God

This tension between already there, but not yet complete is called the [[Eschatological Reservation]]. 

**How is this relevant today?**

It fundamentally changes how people approach life.
This means that we should not passively wait for the end but live and work between what was and what will be.
There is no pressure to create a utopia by yourself, even when things go wrong, because the future is in god's hands. 
This gives people security and self worth/
