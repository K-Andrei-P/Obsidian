What is in the toolbox called transmissionsmechanisms
The processes the ECB takes, to influence the economy. Takes time (time lag) and goes through a chain: Wirkungskette


key interest rates:

- controlling interest rates starts a chain reaction:
	"Wirkungkette"
	1. ECB rises interest rates
	2. Local Banks need to spend more money to have cash on hand
	3. Local Banks pass the inflation rates on to private households and Companies
	4. influences the borrowing, spending and investing of private households and companies
	5. This changes the prices of things
	6. Calms down the economy
- Minimum Reserve Policy
	1. When minimum liquidity rises for the banks
	2. Banks have less money to loan
	3. Loaning becomes more valuable, so they can increase their interest rates, harder to come by
	4. private households and companies cannot loan as much
	5. this changes the prices of things
	6. calms down the economy
- Standing Facilities
		A monetary policy instrument that banks use to manage short-term liquidity on a daily basis. 
		unlike the main refinancing operations that happen weekly
	 - marginal lending facility: banks can lend as much cash as they need through this facility
	 - deposit facility: banks can deposit liquidity, no limit
		the interest rates of the facilities mark the upper and lower limit of bank to bank transactions. thus forming the interest rate canal
		Banks use these facilities to counteract unexpected cash flow at the end of a business day of to manage liquidity imbalances

imagine question: how does increasing key interest rate slow down inflation

the euro system uses increased key interest rates to combat higher inflation rates.

Refinancing operations of banks become more expensive to obtain the liquidity to provide loans to households and businesses.

This costs get passed over to the consumer, making it more expensive for the consumer.

this has mainly three economic aftereffects:
1. decreased credit demand because expensive
2. increased capital costs, making investments less attractive
3. increased saving incentive

lower consumption and lower investment, general decline in goods and services
lowers the pressure for companies to increase prices
thus slowing down inflation
